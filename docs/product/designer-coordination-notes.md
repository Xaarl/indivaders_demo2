# Designer Coordination Notes

## Design Objective

Design should make the product logic easier to understand. Visual experimentation is welcome only when it strengthens the hierarchy from market noise to concrete positioning decisions.

## Preserve

- Arcade/invader/black-hole hero direction.
- Real-game case file direction.
- Interactive sample report direction.
- Workspace as the long-term product direction.
- Evidence/source access as a trust signal.

## Do Not Distort

Do not turn the product into:

- A generic analytics dashboard.
- A playful toy with unclear business value.
- A PDF-style report page.
- A raw database explorer.
- A fake AI assistant.
- A landing page that explains its own design.

## Visual Hierarchy

Landing:

1. Hero promise and primary sample-report CTA.
2. Three benchmark traps.
3. Report decisions.
4. Sample/report conversion.

Sample report:

1. Verdicts.
2. Top actions.
3. Comparables map.
4. Review risks and production reality.
5. Sources.

Workspace:

1. Project status.
2. Current report snapshot.
3. Action progress.
4. Comparables/risks.
5. Source log.
6. Future Market Watch.

## Section Intent

Hero:

- Feel memorable and interactive.
- Communicate noisy signals becoming clearer.
- Avoid visual complexity that hides the headline or CTA.

Case cards:

- Show real games and one sharp trap each.
- Let the user see the lesson quickly.
- Avoid making them look like generic blog cards.

Report deliverables:

- Present decisions, not feature inventory.
- Keep content scannable and concrete.

Interactive report:

- Feel like a decision workspace.
- Prioritize actionable cards, source access, and comparison controls.
- Avoid long paragraph walls.

Intake:

- Quiet, direct, trust-building.
- Make it clear this is a report request, not an account signup.

Workspace:

- Work-grade and organized.
- Treat "future" modules as future.
- Avoid dashboard sprawl.

## Evidence Design

Evidence should be visible without becoming noisy.

Recommended pattern:

- Compact label on claim card when helpful.
- Source button or chip.
- Drawer/detail panel with full source, label, type, retrieval date, and definitions.

Do not place a disclaimer paragraph on every card.

## Copy Density

Use shorter cards where the insight is sharp:

- "Looks comparable"
- "Actually different"
- "Safe lesson"
- "Unsafe assumption"
- "Next action"

Avoid abstract headings such as:

- "Insights"
- "Analytics"
- "Market intelligence"
- "AI recommendation"

## Red Lines

- Do not make Market Watch look live until it is connected to real refresh data.
- Do not make the workspace look like a finished customer portal until persistence exists.
- Do not hide the paid report behind too much free content.
- Do not make game screenshots so small that the case examples lose specificity.
- Do not use internal/product-strategy copy in the UI.

## Designer Deliverables To Request

- Landing hierarchy pass with one primary path: sample report to early report.
- Case-card pattern showing the benchmark trap in one scan.
- Report source/evidence component system.
- Workspace status pattern that distinguishes ready, pending, and future modules.
- Mobile hierarchy pass for hero, report nav, and workspace tabs.
