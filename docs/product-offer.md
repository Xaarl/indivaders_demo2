# Product Offer

Operational summary. The richer product source of truth lives in `docs/product/`.

## Public Offer

Indievaders sells an evidence-labeled Steam Positioning Report for serious solo indie developers and small teams.

The public landing page should stay short, attractive, and action-oriented. It should communicate:

- The report helps teams understand how their Steam page should compete.
- The free lead magnet is one email sample brief.
- The paid report is separate, private, and delivered later.
- The default public price wording is "$49 early Steam Positioning Report".

## Conversion Flow

1. Visitor lands on the public page and sees the Steam positioning promise.
2. Visitor clicks through short real-game case studies.
3. Visitor requests the free email sample brief.
4. The sample brief demonstrates tone, format, and usefulness without exposing paid-report details.
5. Follow-up email can invite qualified teams to ask about the "$49 early Steam Positioning Report".
6. Paid report intake, payment, analysis, and delivery happen outside the public landing page until the product is validated.

## Pricing Wording

Public default:

- "$49 early Steam Positioning Report"
- "Interested in the $49 early Steam Positioning Report?"
- "Start with the free sample brief before paying for your own report."

Avoid:

- vague price ranges,
- discount framing,
- "after validation",
- "pilot validation",
- "ask about pricing".

## Public Content Boundary

Allowed on the public landing page:

- Brand positioning.
- Short hero promise.
- Trust strip.
- Short real-game case cards.
- Compact case detail panels.
- Report questions in plain language.
- Early report summary.
- Free email sample brief CTA.

Keep private:

- Full paid report output.
- Internal scoring labels.
- Detailed methodology.
- Intake logic.
- Automation prompts.
- Research notes.
- Any customer-specific findings.

## Blocked Public Phrases

Do not use these phrases in public landing copy:

- old app
- engine not product
- after validation
- sample pack
- Creator Fit
- Competitors
- Review Audit
- Market contradictions
- local heuristic
- 2D Action Positioning
- pilot validation

## Landing Page Voice

Use concise English copy for developers with serious commercial intent. The tone should feel direct, useful, and premium without becoming corporate.

Avoid:

- Walls of text.
- Prototype or internal wording.
- Fake precision.
- Over-explaining methodology.
- Claims that sound like guaranteed Steam performance.
